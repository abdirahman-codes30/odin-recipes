Latte Recipe

Description

A latte macchiato is a classic Italian coffee drink known for its beautiful layered appearance and smooth, creamy taste. Unlike a traditional latte, the steamed milk is prepared first and then "marked" with a shot of rich espresso, creating distinct layers of milk, coffee, and delicate foam. This recipe produces a balanced, café-style latte macchiato that's perfect for breakfast, an afternoon break, or whenever you want a comforting coffee with a touch of elegance.

Ingredients

  • 1 shot (30 ml / 1 oz) freshly brewed espresso
  • 200 ml (about ¾ cup + 1 tablespoon) whole milk
  • Optional: 1–2 teaspoons vanilla syrup
  • Optional: Sugar or sweetener, to taste
  • Optional: Cocoa powder or cinnamon for garnish

Steps

  1. Brew a fresh shot of espresso using an espresso machine or another strong coffee brewing method.
  2. Pour the milk into a milk pitcher and steam it until it reaches about 60–65°C (140–150°F), creating a smooth, velvety microfoam.
  3. Pour the steamed milk into a tall glass, holding back the thicker foam with a spoon if necessary.
  4. Spoon the milk foam onto the top of the milk, creating a layer approximately 1–2 cm (½ inch) thick.
  5. If using vanilla syrup or another sweetener, stir it gently into the warm milk before adding the espresso.
  6. Slowly pour the espresso through the center of the foam. The espresso will settle between the milk and foam, creating the signature layered effect.
  7. Garnish with a light dusting of cocoa powder or cinnamon if desired.
  8. Serve immediately while hot, allowing the layers to remain visible before stirring, if preferred.
